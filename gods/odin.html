Odin, the Wanderer

Odin is likely the most important figure in all of Norse mythology. He was one of the three gods who created the world from the remains of Ymir, and he is the ruler of the Aesir gods. Despite being the ruler of the gods, he often wanders the world in search for wisdom. It is said that he "sacrificed himself to himself" on the branches of the world tree to gain access to the runes, the source of magic. He also gave up his own eye at the well of Mimir to have a drink of its water, said to give unprecidented knowledge.
